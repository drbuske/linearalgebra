Proposition 5.3.10.

Let \(V\) be a vector space over some field \(K\text{,}\) and consider a set of vectors \(S\subseteq V\text{.}\) Then \(\mathrm{span}(S)\) is the smallest subspace of \(V\) containing \(S\text{.}\) More explicitly, we have:
🔗
  1. The set \(\mathrm{span}(S)\) is a subspace of \(V\text{,}\) and \(S\subseteq\mathrm{span}(S)\text{.}\)
    🔗
  2. If \(W\) is any other subspace of \(V\) such that \(S\subseteq W\text{,}\) then \(\mathrm{span}(S)\subseteq W\text{.}\)
    🔗
🔗

Proof.
  1. To show that \(\mathrm{span}(S)\) is a subspace, first note that \(\zerovec\in\mathrm{span}(S)\text{,}\) because \(\zerovec\) is the empty linear combination. Also, if \(\vvec,\uvec\in\mathrm{span}(S)\text{,}\) then by definition of span, there exist \(\vvec_1,\ldots,\vvec_k,\uvec_1,\ldots,\uvec_\ell\in S\) and \(a_1,\ldots,a_k,b_1,\ldots,b_\ell\in K\) such that
    \begin{align*} \vvec \amp =a_1\vvec_1+\cdots+a_k\vvec_k,\\ \uvec \amp =b_1\uvec_1+\cdots+b_\ell \uvec_\ell. \end{align*}
    Then
    \begin{equation*} \vvec+\uvec=a_1\vvec_1+\cdots+a_k\vvec_k+b_1\uvec_1+\cdots+b_\ell \uvec_\ell, \end{equation*}
    and therefore \(\vvec+\uvec\in\mathrm{span}(S)\text{.}\) It follows that \(\mathrm{span}(S)\) is closed under addition. The proof for scalar multiplication is similar. Finally, every \(\vvec\in S\) is trivially a linear combination of itself, \(\vvec=1\vvec\text{,}\) and therefore \(S\subseteq\mathrm{span}(S)\text{.}\)
    🔗
  2. Consider any other subspace \(W\) of \(V\) such that \(S\subseteq W\text{.}\) To show that \(\mathrm{span}(S)\subseteq W\text{,}\) consider an arbitrary element \(\vvec\in\mathrm{span}(S)\text{.}\) By definition of span, there exist \(\vvec_1,\ldots,\vvec_k\in S\) and \(a_1,\ldots,a_k\in K\) such that \(\vvec=a_1\vvec_1+\cdots+a_k\vvec_k.\) By assumption, \(\vvec_1,\ldots,\vvec_k\in W\text{.}\) Since \(W\) is closed under addition and scalar multiplication, it follows that \(\vvec\in W\text{.}\) Since \(\vvec\) was an arbitrary element of \(\mathrm{span}(S)\text{,}\) it follows that \(\mathrm{span}(S)\subseteq W\text{.}\)
    🔗
