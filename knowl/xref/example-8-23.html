Example 5.2.14.

Consider a sequence of non-zero polynomials \(p_1(x),\ldots,p_k(x)\) of increasing degree, i.e., such that the degree of each \(p_i(x)\) is strictly larger than that of \(p_{i-1}(x)\text{.}\) We show that \(p_1(x),\ldots,p_k(x)\) are linearly independent in the vector space \(\mathbb{P}\text{.}\)
🔗
A polynomial of degree \(n\) cannot be a linear combination of polynomials of degree less than \(n\text{.}\) Therefore, none of the polynomials \(p_1(x),\ldots,p_k(x)\) can be written as a linear combination of earlier polynomials. By Proposition 5.2.13, \(p_1(x),\ldots,p_k(x)\) are linearly independent.
