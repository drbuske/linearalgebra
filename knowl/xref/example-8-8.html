Example 5.1.9.

Let \(\mathbb{P}\) be the set of all polynomials (of any degree) with real coefficients, i.e., expressions of the form \(p(x)=a_nx^n+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+a_1x+a_0 \text{,}\) where \(n\ge 0\) and \(a_0,\ldots,a_n\in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Addition and scalar multiplication of polynomials are defined in the usual way. Then \(\mathbb{P}\) is a vector space.
