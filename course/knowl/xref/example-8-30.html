Example 5.3.6.

Let \(V=\mathrm{Func}_{\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R}}\text{.}\) Recall from calculus that a differential equation is an equation about an unknown function and its derivatives. For example
\begin{equation*} f''=-f \end{equation*}
is a differential equation. The functions \(f(x)=\sin x\text{,}\) \(f(x)=\cos x\text{,}\) and \(f(x)=0\) are examples of solutions of this differential equation. Let \(W\) be the set of all functions that are solutions of the differential equation \(f''=-f\text{.}\) Then \(W\) is a subspace of \(V\text{.}\)
🔗
Proof:The zero function \(f(x)=0\) is a solution of the differential equation, and therefore an element of \(W\text{.}\)
🔗
To show that \(W\) is closed under addition, let \(f,g\in W\) and consider \(h=f+g\text{.}\) Then \(f''=-f\) and \(g''=-g\text{,}\) and therefore \(h''=f''+g''=-f+(-g)=-h\text{.}\) Therefore \(h\in W\text{.}\)
🔗
To show that \(W\) is closed under scalar multiplication, let \(k\in\mathbb{R}\) and \(f\in W\text{,}\) and consider \(h=kf\text{.}\) Then \(f''=-f\text{,}\) and therefore \(h''=kf''=k(-f)=-h\text{.}\) It follows that \(h\in W\text{.}\)
