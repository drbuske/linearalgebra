Example 5.2.12. Linearly independent functions.

In the vector space \(\mathrm{Func}_{\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R}}\) of real-valued functions on the real numbers, we show that the functions \(f(x)=\sin x\) and \(g(x)=\cos x\) are linearly independent.
🔗
Assume \(A\sin x+B\cos x=0\text{.}\) Note that this is an equality of functions, which means that it is true for all \(x\text{.}\)
🔗
In particular, substituting \(x=0\) into the equation, and using the fact that \(\sin 0=0\) and \(\cos 0=1\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} 0=A\sin 0+B\cos 0=A\cdot 0+B\cdot 1=B, \end{equation*}
and therefore \(B=0\text{.}\) On the other hand, substituting \(x=\tfrac{\pi}{2}\) into the equation, and using the fact that \(\sin\tfrac{\pi}{2}=1\) and \(\cos\tfrac{\pi}{2}=0\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} 0=A\sin\tfrac{\pi}{2}+B\cos\tfrac{\pi}{2} =A\cdot 1+B\cdot 0=A, \end{equation*}
and therefore \(A=0\text{.}\) Therefore the equation \(A\sin x+B\cos x=0\) only has the trivial solution \(A=B=0\text{,}\) and it follows that \(\sin x\) and \(\cos x\) are linearly independent.
